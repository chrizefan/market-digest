The thesis (T-003 Defensive Rotation) is confirmed — but XLP is the better expression. > **PM Decision**: ✅ **Accept**. The analyst is right — XLP is the superior defensive instrument. Exit XLV, reallocate to XLP. Thesis T-003 remains active through XLP.

### Challenge 2: IAU — Analyst recommends trimming from 20% to 15%

> **PM**: "Gold just corrected 16% from ATH. Isn't this the buy-the-dip moment rather than trim? What changed?"

> **Analyst**: "Institutional outflows are real (-$1.1B weekly). Momentum is broken short-term. The thesis is intact but short-term headwinds justify de-risking 5%. I'd add back at $84 (IAU) / $4,200/oz if it stabilizes. This isn't abandoning gold — it's acknowledging the correction and creating dry powder."

> **PM Decision**: ⚠️ **Override to HOLD at 20%**. Gold at $4,400 after a -16% correction IS the buy-the-dip moment. Outflows are profit-taking, not structural. The dual macro tailwind (geopolitical + inflation) is too strong. Maintain 20%. Reset the 15% recommendation only if gold breaks $4,200.

### Challenge 3: ITA — New position in a new category

> **PM**: "We have zero defense thesis history. Adding a new asset category during maximum uncertainty is risky. Why not just add to XLE/DBO instead?"

> **Analyst**: "ITA diversifies the war theme away from CTA-crowded oil. Energy longs at 90th percentile vs defense not crowded. ITA has independent spending catalysts (Congressional appropriations). 5% is small enough to be a probe position."

> **PM Decision**: ⚠️ **Override to 0%**. In a regime with this much uncertainty, adding new uncovered positions violates discipline. Our energy exposure (XLE 15% + DBO 5% = 20%) is sufficient war-theme allocation. ITA is a good idea but not now — revisit when we have a formal T-004 thesis. Reallocate the 5% to BIL.

### Challenge 4: XOP, GDX, UUP, DBC — All recommended 0%
