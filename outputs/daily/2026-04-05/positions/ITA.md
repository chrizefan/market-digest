## Bull Case

1. **Direct war beneficiary**: Iran War = surge in defense spending. LMT, RTX, GD (top ITA holdings) all benefiting from increased orders and expedited contracts. This is a secular acceleration, not a one-quarter bump. *Source: sectors/industrials.md defense sub-sector read.*

2. **Bipartisan spending support**: Defense is the only spending category with bipartisan support in current political environment. Supplemental appropriations likely for Iran campaign. *Source: politician-signals.md.*

3. **Non-commodity war play**: ITA offers war premium exposure WITHOUT the CTA crowding risk that plagues energy. Oil longs are at 90th percentile; defense positioning is not nearly as crowded. This is a differentiated exposure within the same macro theme. *Source: cta-positioning.md.*

## Bear Case

1. **Already elevated**: Defense stocks have rallied significantly on Iran War. Buying here is chasing, not leading. *LMT, RTX likely up 15-20% since escalation.*

2. **Peace dividend risk**: If war resolves quickly (Hormuz Protocol success), defense stocks sell off as spending forecasts get cut. Opposite dynamic from energy (where peace = lower but stable earnings). *Source: macro.md Hormuz analysis.*

3. **Sector rotation risk**: In a genuine risk-on rally, money rotates from defensives to cyclicals/growth. ITA would underperform. *Source: us-equities.md factor analysis.*
