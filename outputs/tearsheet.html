The macro regime on April 5, 2026 is best characterized as Transitional/Inflationary with a risk-off lean. The dominant theme is the ongoing Iran War, which has disrupted Strait of Hormuz shipping and driven WTI crude oil above $112 per barrel — levels not seen since 2022. Goldman Sachs estimates a $14-18/barrel geopolitical risk premium is embedded in oil prices. This geopolitical premium is also the primary driver behind gold reaching extraordinary levels (~$4,686/oz), as investors seek safe-haven protection amid both inflation risk and war uncertainty. The March NFP report (+178K vs. +59K consensus) released to closed markets on Good Friday creates a critical inflection point for Monday April 6. The strong beat supports the 'soft landing' narrative and good growth, but with the Fed having already priced out rate cuts this year due to Iran-driven inflation, the data will primarily reinforce the higher-for-longer stance. Importantly, wage growth at 3.5% y/y is cooling to its lowest since May 2021 — this limits the upside rate pressure and reduces the risk of a Fed hike. The combination suggests a gap-up open in equities on Monday, but with limited follow-through until war uncertainty clears. Note: the NFP headline (+178K) was partially flattered by ~35K healthcare workers returning from a strike; underlying organic job growth is closer to ~130-145K — still solid but not as dramatic. This inaugural positioning is intentionally defensive and diversified. The large gold allocation (20%, max allowed) reflects both the structural demand environment and the war risk premium — gold has a structural bid from central bank buying, de-dollarization trends, and the Iran War, making it effective in multiple scenarios. Energy (12% XLE + 5% DBO) captures the direct oil premium through both equity and commodity exposure. Defensive sectors (XLP 8%, XLV 10%) provide equity market participation without maximum cyclical risk. A 45% allocation to cash equivalents (BIL, SHY, and cash) reflects the high uncertainty and the significant tail risk of either a war escalation or a sudden de-escalation. The SPX is in negative YTD territory and the curve has un-inverted to +52bps — suggesting markets are pricing persistent inflation over recession for now.
Key Risks
  • Iran War de-escalation: A credible ceasefire would sharply reverse gold (-5 to -10%) and oil (-15 to -25%); would hurt IAU, DBO, and XLE positions significantly. Exit trigger: WTI closing sustainably below $80.
  • NFP Monday gap + hawkish repricing: Strong jobs data could push 10Y yields above 4.50%, pressuring risk assets even amid good growth data. Watch for 10Y > 4.55% sustained as a signal to increase cash further.
  • Yen carry unwind: USD/JPY at 159.63 is an extended carry trade. A BOJ surprise or flight-to-safety event could trigger a rapid yen-strengthening flash sell-off across global risk assets — similar to August 2024. Monitor USD/JPY < 155 as early warning.
  • Fed rate hike risk: If Iran-driven inflation forces the Fed to hike rather than hold, all risk assets broadly sell off. Wage data (3.5% y/y cooling) currently makes this unlikely but not impossible.
  • Escalation beyond Hormuz: If the Iran War widens to involve other regional actors or disrupts additional supply chains, oil could spike further — positive for XLE/DBO short-term but ultimately deflationary via demand destruction.
