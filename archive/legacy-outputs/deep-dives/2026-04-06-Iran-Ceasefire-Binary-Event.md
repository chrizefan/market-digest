## D. Macro Context

### Why This Binary Is Genuinely Binary

Most binary events have known resolution dates (earnings, FOMC) with some central tendency. This ceasefire binary has:

1. **No known resolution mechanism**: Trump hasn't communicated what "proof" of Hormuz opening would satisfy him. Is one tanker passing through enough? Full traffic? A signed agreement?

2. **Intermediary uncertainty**: The mediators (Pakistan, Egypt, Turkey) have no enforcement power. Even if a deal is "reached," implementation could drag for days/weeks.

3. **Iran's rational calculus**: Iran's oil exports through Hormuz are already disrupted. Compliance = giving up leverage. Non-compliance = strikes. Neither path is obviously better for Iran's regime.

4. **Trump optionality**: Trump could declare "partial success" if Iran makes any gesture (reopen to some traffic) and claim victory without formal ceasefire. This middle path would give a mixed market signal.

### Historical Precedent — Oil War Endings

| Conflict | Resolution | Oil Reaction | Duration of Decline |
|----------|-----------|-------------|-------------------|
| Gulf War 1990-91 | Rapid military victory | -30% within 2 months | 45 days |
| Iraq War 2003 | US victory | -15% in 3 months | 60 days |
| Libya 2011 | Ceasefire | -10% spike then recover | 30 days |
| Russia-Ukraine 2022 | No resolution | -40% from peak over 6 months | N/A (ongoing) |

**Pattern**: War endings cause swift, violent commodity reversals. The energy market is NOT slow to price resolution — CTAs unwind mechanically. The Libya 2011 precedent (smaller disruption, $10-15 war premium) is most analogous.

### The "Inflation Still Hot" Wildcard

Even in ceasefire scenario, CPI Thursday (April 10) shows ~3.8% YoY inflation driven by oil. A ceasefire doesn't immediately reduce consumer gas prices — it takes 4-8 weeks for lower crude to reach pump prices. The Fed will not immediately cut rates after a ceasefire. This limits the equity upside from de-escalation: bull case is +3-5%, not +10%.
